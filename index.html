About Me

I am Mukelani N. Sindana, a 20-year-old Full Stack Web Developer in training studying at iHub Africa, based in Gauteng, South Africa.

I didn't come from a background that handed me everything so I learned early that if I want something I have to go and get it myself. Web development is that thing for me right now.

I started coding because I needed something that was mine. Something I could build a future on — not just for me but for the family I want to have one day.

What drives me is building things that actually matter — charity platforms, community pages and experiences that make people feel something.

I am quick to adapt, I don't mind making mistakes and I don't pretend to know everything. I just keep going.

Bakery Project

A fully functional bakery website with product listings, shopping cart, and checkout process.

Case Study

Problem: I needed to build an e-commerce website where users could browse products, add items to cart, and complete a checkout process.

What I built: A complete bakery website with dynamic product listings, a functioning shopping cart with quantity controls, total calculation, and a multi-step checkout form.

Tools used: HTML, CSS, Vanilla JavaScript, localStorage for cart persistence, GitHub, GitHub Pages.

What I learned: How to manage complex state with a shopping cart, calculate totals dynamically, validate form inputs, and persist user data with localStorage across sessions.

What I am proud of: The cart functionality works smoothly — items can be added, removed, quantities updated, and the total recalculates instantly. The form validation ensures data integrity.

What I would do differently: Add actual payment integration, implement user accounts with order history, and add product filtering and sorting options.
